What is UNIHACK?

UNIHACK is a student-orientated hackathon in Melbourne, organised by the I.T. student clubs from the University of Melbourne and Monash University, Clayton.

Open to all uni students

You do not have to be a Melbourne or Monash student to participate, UNIHACK is open to all university and TAFE students in Victoria.

Mentored by experts

Receive advice on your idea and be mentored by people in the I.T. industry - from the big companies to the small startups in Australia.

Build an app in just 24 hours

Teams have only 24 hours to build an app or prototypes. It can be for any platform - it just needs to be working on Presentation Day.

UNIHACK Theme: Discovery

This year's UNIHACK theme is discovery. Teams must incorporate the UNIHACK theme into their app or prototype, but how teams interpret it is up to them.

FAQs

Who can participate at UNIHACK?

UNIHACK is open to all students currently enrolled in a Victorian university or TAFE (not just those in the University of Melbourne or Monash University). The event is open to students of all majors and minors - so you do not need to have a Computer Science or Software Engineering degree to participate.

How many people can I have in a team?

You can have up to four people in our team. You can also work on a project by yourself (i.e. a team of one person), but we encourage you collaborate with others to get the full hackathon experience.

What do I need to bring?

You will need to bring a laptop, your mobile phone and chargers for both. It is also suggested that you bring a sleeping bag, pillow and a change of clothes for the next day. Also, don't forget to bring yourself and your teammates!

Please DO NOT bring illegal drugs, alcohol, firearms or any other weapon.

Will there be any internet access during UNIHACK?

Yes, there will be wireless internet available during UNIHACK - courtesy from our location partners, York Butter Factory. You will be given the wireless password when you arrive.

Can I start working on my project before UNIHACK?

No. Teams should do all the development work during the 24 hour period. Because of the compressed time frame, we do allow teams to research the tools, concepts and ideas of your project beforehand.

Do I need to stay for the entire 24 hours?

No, you do not. We understand if people would like to sleep on a comfortable bed instead of the floor. Participants are able to stay and leave during the 24 hours - just make sure your team knows where you are.

Why are the presentations held on a Monday night?

Because we wanted to give teams the Sunday to rest before presenting their idea, because doing anything - even pitching your idea to the judges - after staying up for 24 hours is not fun. We should know.

Who owns what gets made at UNIHACK?

Any intellectual property (e.g. code, images, audio) that you and/or your team create during UNIHACK belongs to you and/or your team.

What do I do if I have more questions?

Feel free to email your questions to fits@monashclubs.org. Alternatively, you can send them through our Facebook page and Twitter account.
